Yugabyte Internship

TIMELINE

Summer 2020 Internship (June '20 - Aug '20)

ROLE

UX Designer
Wireframing, Prototyping

OVERVIEW

I spent the course of my Summer 2020 internship at Yugabyte working on the redesign of aspects of the website. In particular, I worked on the News page, the Success Stories page, and the Platform page redesign. In doing so, the goal was to update the site to reflect the new brand traits.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The pages on the Yugabyte website do not represent the product and the direction that the product is going in, and should do so while taking into account the Yugabyte Brand Traits.

DISCOVER

Background

The focus over the course of my internship was the News page, the Success Stories pages, and the Platform page. Those pages are key pages to update and highlight because they play an important role in the projection of what Yugabyte is what what kind of product they offer to their customers, as well as the kinds of successes they have had.

Existing Interfaces

IDEATION

Website Wireframes

DESIGN

Website UI Screens

From those wireframes, I took into account the updated color scheme for Yugabyte and incorporated those into creating a high-fidelity mockup.

News & Press Releases

By separating the News section and the Press Release sections, the user can quickly switch between the two by toggling at the top of the page so they can see what others are saying about Yugabyte. Additionally, there would be featured articles for specific news that would want to be highlighted.

Platform

The Platform page is meant to communicate what the Yugabyte product does. Keeping it concise through higlighting the key features was a main goal, in order to draw visitors into not only understanding the product but how it would help them.

Success Stories

Through the Success Stories page, the goal was to highlight the key aspect of what draws the customers to Yugabyte: why they selected Yugabyte, and what their results are. The user would have a stuck tab that follows them as they scroll that emphasizes the "why" behind the customer's story with Yugabyte, to drive that point home.

TAKEAWAYS

Reflections

I think this summer internship gave me a good opportunity to better understand client-related products, and how to approach design from the perspective of think of current as well as potential future customers. While I think in a non-pandemic, non-remote internship, perhaps the way the thought process behind these designs might have changed, I believe it was also a good example and practice of my communication and capacity to work with others.
