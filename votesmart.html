VoteSmart

TIMELINE

VoteSmart was created as part of a semester-long course as an individual project at the University of Michigan.

ROLE

Product Designer
User Research, Visual Design, Prototyping, User Testing

OVERVIEW

VoteSmart is a mobile app that keeps users up to date on the day-to-day political occurrences as well as the ballot during an election year.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Voters do not have technology that incentivizes them to vote or keep up with politics on an ongoing basis.

DISCOVER

Background

U.S. elections have a history of a low voter turnout. Even in major presidential elections, voter turnout has been at 60% of the eligible voters. Reasons that people do not vote can range from their lack of knowledge about what is on the ballot for the election to just not being interested or invested in politics.

The goal of VoteSmart is to increase the number of voters who participate in U.S. elections. By encouraging users to stay up to date with politics between elections, they can feel more incentivized to vote on issues and candidates that they have learned about or have an interest in.

User Research

From my user research and competitive analysis, I learned just how wide the scope of this topic is. With a topic such as voter education and awareness, there is so much information that could be covered, like voting day information, polling information, community reactions and opinions regarding bills and representatives, among other things. However, users stressed the importance of making sure that the features were not overwhelming. They wanted the option of being able to use features at some point, even if they may not use it all the time. I chose to focus on what interviewees wanted to see the most - non-partisan, accurate information that would allow them to keep an eye on what was going on in politics so they can use that information to aid them in making their choices during an election.

IDEATION

Phone App Sketches

When sketching ideas for the app, I wanted to incorporate my ideas for how users could learn more about bills, who their representatives were, and how they would or could set up their ballot to vote in an election.

Phone App Wireframes

DESIGN

Phone App User Flow

The user flow for the mobile out lays out all of the features that the user may interact with alongside where they are within the app itself. It provides a guide for how features can be found as well as how they are organized.

Phone App UI Screens

When creating the high fidelity screens for vote smart, I focused on expanding certain screens from the wireframes that I thought were the most important to expand upon and graphically detail.

Daily Schedule and Bills

The first feature allows the user to see what is happening in Congress that day, whether it is what bills are being introduced or voted on, what events their congressperson is holding, or what hearings are taking place in subcomittees. To learn more about specific bills, users can not only read a summary, but how representatives have voted on them. They can, if they want, read the full text of the bill. Each of the bills are tagged by the topics of relevance, through which users can see bills that are similarly tagged.

Representatives Profile

The user can also view information about the representatives in Congress and the Senate. They can read more about each representative in their bio, see how they have voted on issues and bills, and view (and RSVP) to events that a representative has coming up.

Organizing Your Ballot

Before filling out their ballot, the user can learn more about candidates and propositions that are up for voting. They can filter candidates by political party before reading about the candidates or propositions and their platforms. Users can select how they would like to vote for each candidate or measure and use the saved information to vote either at the polling station or by mail.

TAKEAWAYS

Reflection

Reflecting on this project, what I think was difficult to understand was just how broad of a problem was trying to be solved. Things that were also brought up through interviews were desires to increase voter registration, finding polling stations, and other things more relating to elections. There are many directions in which to take a topic such as politics and voter education and engagement. People are also selective about the kinds things they are looking for, so even trying to cover a range of topics means that some users would not use all of the features of the app.
